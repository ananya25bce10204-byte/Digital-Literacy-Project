# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which scammers trick individuals into transferring money through digital payment apps. This type of fraud often targets smartphone users who rely on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm for daily transactions.

A typical scam begins when the fraudster contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller in an online marketplace. Step-by-step, the scammer builds trust and then sends a “collect request” or a fake payment link. The victim is misled into believing they will receive money, but instead, they are asked to enter their UPI PIN. Once the PIN is entered, money is instantly debited from their account. In some cases, scammers also use QR codes or malicious links to gain access.

The usual targets are students, elderly people, or individuals unfamiliar with digital payment security practices. People in urgent situations or those expecting payments are especially vulnerable.

The consequences can be severe. Victims may lose significant amounts of money, and recovery is often difficult due to the instant nature of UPI transactions. Additionally, victims may experience stress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital systems.

This case highlights the importance of awareness—users should never share their UPI PIN, approve unknown requests, or trust unsolicited payment links.
