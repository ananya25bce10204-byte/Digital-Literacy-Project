# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where scammers trick people into transferring money through digital payment apps. This fraud often targets smartphone users who rely on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm for daily transactions. A typical scam starts when the fraudster contacts the victim by phone, SMS, or social media. They might pretend to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer or seller in an online marketplace. Step by step, the scammer builds trust and sends a “collect request” or a fake payment link. The victim is led to believe they will receive money, but instead, they must enter their UPI PIN. Once the PIN is entered, money is quickly taken from their account. In some cases, scammers also use QR codes or harmful links to gain access. The usual targets include students, elderly people, or those who are not familiar with digital payment security practices. People in urgent situations or those expecting payments are particularly vulnerable. The consequences can be severe. Victims may lose a lot of money, and recovering it is often hard because of the quick nature of UPI transactions. Additionally, victims may face stress, anxiety, and a loss of trust in digital systems. This case shows the importance of being aware. Users should never share their UPI PIN, approve unknown requests, or trust unsolicited payment links.
