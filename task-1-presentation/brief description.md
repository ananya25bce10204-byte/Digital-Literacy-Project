Digital Literacy refers to the skills and knowledge required to effectively use digital technologies in everyday life. It involves the ability to find, evaluate, 
create, and communicate information through digital platforms in a safe, responsible, and efficient manner. A digitally literate person can navigate the internet, 
use various software tools, and critically assess online content for accuracy and reliability.

An important part of digital literacy is following safe internet practices, such as creating strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication (2FA), being 
cautious of phishing attempts, using secure websites, and keeping devices and software updated to prevent security risks.

Additionally, digital literacy includes building a professional online presence, which represents your digital footprint. This means sharing appropriate content, 
showcasing your skills and achievements, and maintaining a positive and credible image that can be viewed by employers, clients, and others in the digital world.
